First Day Route

A simple sequence of actions to help you settle in during your first session.

1

Complete the opening tutorial

Follow the tutorial prompts to learn basic movement, interaction and the town layout. This unlocks your core tools and area access.

2

Learn the basic controls and town layout

Take a moment to explore the starting area. Understand where shops, bulletin boards, and your home base are located.

3

Pick your first hobby or activity

Choose from cooking, fishing, gardening or crafting. Each hobby opens up new interactions and collectible progress.

4

Check available notices and possible code sources

Look for in-game notices, community boards, or official social channels. Code tracking will be available on this site once verified.

5

Gather common materials

Collect wood, stone, fruit and other basic resources you see around. These are used in many early recipes and requests.

6

Save useful items for recipes, gifts and future tasks

Keep a storage habit from day one. Many items that seem common now may be requested later or used in craft combinations.

This is a suggested general route, not an official game walkthrough. Individual progress may vary.

Beginner Priority Cards

Four key areas to focus on during your first week.

Learn the town systems

Take time to understand the shop system, inventory management, and NPC interaction basics. Rushing through these can lead to early confusion.

Build a simple daily habit

Establish a short repeating routine early. Consistency matters more than efficiency in the first few days. A few focused actions are better than scattered exploration.

Avoid wasting rare items

Some materials are harder to find than they first appear. If an item seems limited or takes effort to obtain, store it until you know its use.

Track useful discoveries

Keep personal notes about fish locations, gift reactions, and recipe results. These will help you when the guide database expands with verified data.

Common Beginner Mistakes

A few things new players often do that are worth avoiding.

Using every item immediately

It is easy to use rare materials as soon as you get them. Some are needed later for specific recipes, requests, or gift preferences. Practice storing first, using later.

Ignoring daily routines

Skipping daily requests or material collection can slow down progress over time. Even five minutes of routine tasks helps maintain steady advancement.

Not tracking useful materials

Many items that seem common now may become important later. Keeping a simple log of what you find and where helps avoid repeated searching.

Believing unverified code lists

Code lists shared online may be outdated or incorrect. Always verify codes through official sources or trusted community channels before attempting to redeem them.

Trying to complete everything at once

Heartopia is designed to be played at a comfortable pace. Attempting to finish all available content in one session can lead to burnout. Focus on one or two goals per day.
